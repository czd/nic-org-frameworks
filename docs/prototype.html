Strategic Research & Frameworks

Making organizations machine-readable

A body of strategic frameworks exploring how organizations and individuals must restructure for the AI-native era — where the bottleneck is no longer technology, but organizational design.

The technology revolution is here. The human one is about to begin.
Core Thesis

AI readiness is an organizational design problem — not a technology adoption problem.

Most organizations are drowning in AI-generated volume while lacking the structures to ensure correctness. The frameworks I've developed address the gap between what AI can do and what organizations are equipped to capture.

The Coordination Crisis

As individual output multiplies, legacy team structures create exponential coordination costs. The mathematics of communication pathways makes this inevitable, not optional.

Volume vs. Correctness

AI has made production volume free. The only remaining scarcity is correctness — the ability to recognize whether output is strategically sound, architecturally coherent, and worth shipping.

The Intent Gap

Organizations have never needed to write down their values and decision logic. Humans filled the gaps through osmosis. Agents cannot. This gap is now the primary bottleneck.

Ambition Expansion

The goal is not efficiency — doing the same with fewer people. It's expansion — using the multiplier to pursue missions that were previously impossible at any headcount.

Framework Library

Six interconnected frameworks for the AI-native transition.

Each framework addresses a specific layer of the transition — from team architecture to individual skill development to autonomous system governance.

AI-Native Teams
How organizations must restructure their units of work — moving from departments to high-context, AI-augmented small teams optimized for correctness over volume.
Intent Engineering
The discipline of making organizational purpose, values, and decision boundaries machine-readable — going beyond prompts and context to encode what agents should want.
Frontier Operations
Five competencies for working at the expanding boundary between human judgment and machine execution. Skills that expire every 90 days as the capability frontier moves.
Talent Identification
Protocols for identifying individuals who can direct AI rather than be directed by it — replacing legacy performance rubrics with diagnostic missions.
Autonomy Levels
A five-level progression model for AI-driven development — from assisted autocomplete to fully autonomous production systems.
Strategic Reorganization
The operational playbook for transitioning legacy organizations into federated, high-autonomy structures — including the economic case and implementation mandates.
Two Audiences

The frameworks serve two distinct groups with connected needs.

Track 1

Leaders & Organizations

General managers who know AI should be transforming their organizations but have no playbook for moving from hype to results. The bottleneck isn't technology — it's that their company's purpose and decision logic were never written down.

→ Strategic workshops for leadership teams
→ AI readiness assessments with prioritized roadmaps
→ 1:1 coaching for frontier leadership skills
Track 2

Individuals & Professionals

Mid-career professionals with real domain expertise who feel the ground shifting. They're using AI tools but sense that producing faster isn't the same as staying relevant. They need a framework for continuous recalibration.

→ Public workshops with self-assessment frameworks
→ Frontier skills bootcamp for applied competencies
→ Career navigation coaching for transitions
The Bridge

The two tracks reinforce each other.

Leaders who attend workshops ask how to help their people navigate the shift. Individuals who develop frontier skills become the talent leaders need. Each track generates demand for the other.
